Trekking Difficulty Levels

Choose a trek that matches your fitness level

Easy Treks

3-5 hours walking daily, altitudes below 3,500m. Suitable for beginners and families.

Examples: Ghorepani Poon Hill, Dhampus Sarangkot

Moderate Treks

5-7 hours walking daily, altitudes up to 4,500m. Requires good fitness level.

Examples: ABC, Langtang, Lower Dolpo

Challenging Treks

7-8+ hours walking, high passes above 5,000m. Requires excellent fitness.

Examples: EBC, Annapurna Circuit, Manaslu

Expedition Treks

Technical climbing, extreme altitudes. For experienced trekkers with guide.

Examples: Three Passes, Upper Dolpo, Kanchenjunga

Altitude & Safety Information

Essential tips for safe trekking in high altitudes

Acclimatization

Climb high, sleep low. Take rest days every 3-4 days above 3,000m.

Hydration

Drink 3-4 liters of water daily. Avoid alcohol and smoking.

AMS Prevention

Recognize symptoms: headache, nausea, dizziness. Descend if symptoms worsen.

Travel Insurance

Mandatory coverage for helicopter evacuation (up to 6,000m).

Our guides are trained in wilderness first aid and carry comprehensive first aid kits and oxygen.
