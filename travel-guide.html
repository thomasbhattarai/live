Visa Information

Everything you need to know about Nepal visa requirements

Visa on Arrival

Available at Tribhuvan International Airport and land border crossings for most nationalities.

15/30/90 Days
$30-$125
  • Valid passport (6+ months)
  • Passport-sized photo
  • Completed arrival card
  • Visa fee in USD cash

Online Visa Application

Apply in advance through Nepal's official immigration website for faster processing.

Online Portal
e-Visa
  • Apply at: online.nepalimmigration.gov.np
  • Processing: 3-5 working days
  • Print approval letter
  • Present at immigration

Visa Extensions

Extend your visa at Department of Immigration in Kathmandu or Pokhara Immigration Office.

Up to 150 Days
$3/day
  • Minimum 15 days extension
  • Apply before visa expires
  • Passport photos required
  • Overstay penalty: $5/day

Visa-free countries: India (no restrictions), Bhutan, Maldives (90 days)

Note: Requirements may change. Check official sources before travel.

Best Time to Visit Nepal

Seasonal guide for different activities

Spring (March-May)

Weather: Mild temperatures, blooming rhododendrons

Best for: Trekking, mountain views, cultural tours

Temperature: 10-25°C (hills), 20-35°C (Terai)

Festivals: Holi, Nepali New Year

Summer/Monsoon (June-August)

Weather: Hot, humid, afternoon rains

Best for: Rain-shadow areas (Mustang, Dolpo), indoor activities

Temperature: 15-30°C (hills), 25-40°C (Terai)

Considerations: Leeches, flight delays possible

Autumn (September-November)

Weather: Clear skies, stable weather, festivals

Best for: All activities - peak trekking season

Temperature: 5-20°C (hills), 15-30°C (Terai)

Festivals: Dashain, Tihar, Chhath

Winter (December-February)

Weather: Cold mornings/nights, clear days

Best for: Low-altitude treks, cultural tours, wildlife

Temperature: -10 to 10°C (mountains), 5-20°C (hills)

Considerations: Some high passes closed

Local Transport in Nepal

Getting around Nepal - options and costs

Domestic Flights

Fastest way to reach remote areas. Multiple daily flights to Pokhara, Lukla, Jomsom, Bharatpur.

Cost: $80-200 one way

Airlines: Buddha Air, Yeti Airlines, Tara Air

Tourist Buses

Comfortable coaches with AC, reserved seats. Kathmandu-Pokhara (6-7 hrs), Kathmandu-Chitwan (5-6 hrs).

Cost: $10-20 per sector

Booking: Through travel agencies

Taxis & Ride-Sharing

Metered taxis in cities (use meter or agree price first). Pathao and Tootle apps available in Kathmandu.

Cost: $2-10 within city

Tip: Install ride apps for better rates

Car Rental

Self-drive available (international license needed). Chauffeur-driven cars recommended for tourists.

Cost: $40-80/day with driver

Included: Fuel, driver, insurance

SIM Cards & Internet

Stay connected during your Nepal trip

Nepal Telecom (NTC)

Coverage: Best nationwide coverage including remote trekking areas

SIM Cost: Free with passport copy

Data Packages: 1GB/day for 7 days: ~$3

Where to Buy: Airport, NTC offices, authorized dealers

Ncell

Coverage: Excellent in cities and towns, limited in remote areas

SIM Cost: Free with passport copy

Data Packages: 2GB for 7 days: ~$3.50

Where to Buy: Airport, Ncell centers, mobile shops

Internet Access

WiFi: Available in most hotels, restaurants, cafes in cities

Trekking Areas: Limited WiFi, paid service ($2-5/hour)

Internet Speed: Moderate in cities, slow in mountains

Tip: Get local SIM for better connectivity

Electricity & Charging

Voltage: 220V, 50Hz (Type C, D, M plugs)

Power Cuts: Rare in cities, common in villages

Trekking Lodges: Charging available (often extra fee)

Tip: Bring universal adapter, power bank

Essential Travel Tips

1. Health: Drink bottled/boiled water. Consider altitude sickness medication for trekking.

2. Safety: Generally safe for tourists. Take normal precautions with valuables.

3. Culture: Remove shoes before entering temples/homes. Dress modestly.

4. Bargaining: Acceptable in markets, not in fixed-price shops.

5. Emergency: Police: 100, Ambulance: 102, Tourist Police: 1144
