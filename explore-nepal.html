About Nepal

Land of diversity and natural beauty

Culture & Heritage

Nepal is a melting pot of over 125 ethnic groups, each with unique traditions, languages, and festivals. The country is predominantly Hindu and Buddhist, with ancient temples and monasteries dotting the landscape. Major festivals like Dashain, Tihar, and Holi showcase Nepal's vibrant cultural tapestry.

Geography & Climate

Nepal's terrain ranges from the lowland Terai plains at 60m elevation to Mount Everest at 8,848m. This dramatic variation creates diverse climate zones: tropical in the south, temperate in the middle hills, and arctic in the high Himalayas. The country experiences four distinct seasons, with spring and autumn being the best for tourism.

History

Nepal's history dates back to the Neolithic age. The Kathmandu Valley was the center of the ancient Newar civilization. United under King Prithvi Narayan Shah in 1768, Nepal maintained its independence throughout colonial rule. The country transitioned to a federal democratic republic in 2008, embracing modernity while preserving ancient traditions.

UNESCO World Heritage Sites

Nepal's cultural and natural treasures recognized by UNESCO

Kathmandu Durbar Square

Historic palace complex showcasing Newar architecture, temples, and courtyards dating back to the 12th century.

Patan Durbar Square

One of the oldest Buddhist cities, famous for its fine craftsmanship, metalwork, and wood carvings.

Bhaktapur Durbar Square

Preserved medieval city with traditional pottery and weaving industries, and magnificent palace architecture.

Swayambhunath Stupa

Ancient religious complex atop a hill, also known as Monkey Temple, dating back over 2,000 years.

Boudhanath Stupa

One of the largest spherical stupas in Nepal, center of Tibetan Buddhism with monasteries and prayer wheels.

Pashupatinath Temple

Sacred Hindu temple complex on the banks of Bagmati River, dedicated to Lord Shiva.

Changu Narayan Temple

Oldest Hindu temple in Nepal, dating to the 4th century, with exquisite stone, wood, and metal carvings.

Lumbini

Birthplace of Lord Buddha, featuring the Mayadevi Temple, sacred garden, and monasteries from Buddhist countries.

Sagarmatha National Park

Home to Mount Everest and other high peaks, with unique Sherpa culture and rare wildlife like snow leopards.

Chitwan National Park

First national park in Nepal, protecting endangered species like Bengal tigers and one-horned rhinoceros.
